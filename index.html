How does this program work?

  • Sign up with the Waste Bank: This enables us open a Waste Bank Account (WBA) for an individual or institution that is interested in saving with the Waste Bank. All records will be properly aligned through this medium and accuracy is easily achievable. On signing up, you get 1,000 free points.
  • Deposit: You can deposit your recyclable materials directly with the Waste Bank or call our agent to transfer the waste you’ve accrued. Our aspiration is to one day recycle both organic and inorganic waste, but we are unable to achieve that at this time. However, we currently accept the following items;
    1. Aluminium Cans
    2. Plastic Bowls
    3. Plastic Covers
    4. Plastic Bottles
    5. Plastic Buckets
    6. Plastic Chairs
    7. Pure Water Sachets
    8. Used Paper
    9. Used Cartons
    10. Glass Bottles (Amber, Green, Flint) Our team is doing their best to improve on the list of items we accept.
  • Obtain Points: By depositing your recyclables with the Waste Bank, it gets weighed in kilograms and points are allocated, indicative of the value of recyclable materials deposited. 2 points is equal to 1 Naira.
  • Withdrawal: A customer can request for a withdrawal after a minimum period of 4 months from the day of the first deposit. Based on the Product selected by a customer, withdrawals will be credited directly to the customer either in cash or as a product; provided customers meet the eligibility criteria for withdrawals.

Why this program?

It has been discovered that of all the waste we create globally, only 10% of it gets recycled; the remaining 90% of it ends up getting burnt, in landfills, or in our oceans. This in itself is a global pandemic resulting in environmental pollution, climate change, among others. Very few countries have been able to solve the waste crisis in their locality; Sweden is a powerhouse in the recycling value chain with them being able to recycle over 90% of all the waste they create. They have built a strong waste management system to the point that they even have to import waste to keep the system running.
This however is an isolated experience because in Nigeria and other countries of Africa, waste management is a serious crisis resulting in dirty and unhealthy spaces. With the Waste Bank, we are committed to making the experience of Sweden the story in other parts of the world, but starting from Africa—specifically, Port Harcourt, Nigeria. We are constantly studying systems and environmental differences with the goal of proffering precise solutions to this pandemic.
We believe in a cleaner, greener tomorrow in which we have zero waste and we are working with this belief in a sustainable fashion. With the Waste Bank, we are introducing the concept of baking into waste management: a financial system powered by waste, where individuals and institutions get to save their waste the same way they save money with a financial institution.
We are designing a community-focused system; in that the Waste Bank in a particular community will be set up and operated in relation to the circumstances of that community. Our focus is building a system that ensures every community we get into adopts our values and integrates proper waste management and recycling as a culture.

What are we offering you?

Employee plan;

designed for corporate organizations to help cover some parts of employee remuneration.

Income Plan;

designed for people who want actual cash for their waste.

Student Plan;

designed to help with tuition fees.

Investment Plan;

designed to help individuals meet up financial milestones.

Commodity Plan;

designed to help people get basic supplies and household items.

These product offerings help the Waste Bank know the manner in which value is appropriated to our customers. A customer is at liberty to switch from one product offering to another but with due consultation with management.

What do I benefit from this program?

This program is designed to make people gain additional support in form of income or value and is such that with a very strong commitment, can improve your finances greatly. Points are to be converted to value but with strict adherence to the product offering selected by a customer. It has been noticed that there exists a very high degree of fluctuation in the value of recyclable materials and so as to ensure a level of price stability, points are allocated on the basis of carefully evaluated averages which have been derived in the best interest of our customers. Records of points obtained and activity will on a monthly basis be updated in the customers’ record at the end of every transaction, and sent out to customers to help ensure the highest levels of accountability possible. Beyond the financial gains from the program, we will in line with our mission of creating a sustainability mentality, introduce our valued customers to regular and up-to-date information on the environment, climate change, and recycling. For further details on the benefits, you can speak to a representative.

What is my contribution to Society?

By saving your waste with the bank, you are automatically making your environment a better, cleaner and greener place. Also, 2% of a customer’s value will be set aside to enable Beljat Cycle carry out strategic community based programs so as to make possible the desired social and economic change. We will as a future program and an offshoot of the Waste Bank, introduce Beljat Lab; a system to provide free resources, education and training needed in the future work place.

What more can I do?

Besides being an active customer with the Waste Bank you can support us by telling others about the Waste Bank because it is our desire that everybody recycles. Also, donations to help us expand our operational activities will be graciously accepted. Thank you for banking with us…
